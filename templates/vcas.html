Finger Gestures and Their Functions

Let us delve into the specific finger gestures and what they represent in the virtual calculator:

1.Drawing Mode (Thumb and Index Finger Up)

Gesture: Raise your thumb and index finger while keeping the other fingers down.

Function: This activates the drawing mode. You can draw on the virtual canvas by moving your hand, and the path of your index finger will be traced on the screen.

Explanation: The program checks if the thumb and index finger are up (self.fingers[0] == self.fingers[1] == 1) and ensures that only two fingers are up (sum(self.fingers) == 2).

2.Eraser Mode (Thumb and Middle Finger Up)

Gesture: Raise your thumb and middle finger while keeping the other fingers down.

Function: This switches to eraser mode. You can erase parts of your drawing by moving your hand over the areas you wish to remove.

Explanation: The program detects this gesture by checking if the thumb and middle finger are up (self.fingers[0] == self.fingers[2] == 1) with a total of two fingers raised.

3.Clear Canvas (Thumb and Pinky Finger Up)

Gesture: Raise your thumb and pinky finger while keeping the other fingers down.

Function: This clears the entire canvas, giving you a fresh start.

Explanation: The program recognizes this gesture when the thumb and pinky finger are up (self.fingers[0] == self.fingers[4] == 1) and only two fingers are raised.

4.Analyze Equation (All Fingers Up)

Gesture: Raise all five fingers.

Function: This triggers the analysis of the drawn equation using Google's Generative AI model. The AI interprets the mathematical expression you've drawn, solves it, and provides a step-by-step explanation.

Explanation: The condition sum(self.fingers) == 5 indicates that all fingers are raised.

5.Reset Drawing Points (Thumb, Index, and Middle Finger Up)

Gesture: Raise your thumb, index finger, and middle finger.

Function: This resets the drawing points without clearing the canvas, which can be useful if you want to reposition your hand or start a new stroke.

Explanation: The program checks for this gesture with self.fingers[0] == self.fingers[1] == self.fingers[2] == 1 and a total of three fingers raised.
